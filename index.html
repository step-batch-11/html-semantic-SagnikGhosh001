Rooftop Gardens Are Changing Cities

By Maria Lopez

Published: March 15, 2026

Introduction

Urban farming is transforming unused city spaces into productive green zones. Rooftop gardens are leading this change.

Why Rooftop Gardens Matter

  1. Cities face food insecurity and environmental stress. Rooftop gardens reduce heat and improve air quality.
  2. Urban farms can reduce city temperatures by 2°C.
  3. Average yield per rooftop: 500 kg per year.

Real-World Example

In 2024, the SkyGrow Project launched in New York City.

Project Funding

The initial investment was $250,000.

Conclusion

  1. Rooftop farming is more than a trend.
  2. it is a sustainable movement.
